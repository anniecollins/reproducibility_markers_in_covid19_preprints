KLEIN COVERINGS OF GENUS 2 CURVES

arXiv:1904.05962v2 [math.AG] 12 Nov 2019

PAWEL BOROÌWKA, ANGELA ORTEGA

Abstract. We investigate the geometry of eÌtale 4 : 1 coverings of smooth complex genus 2 curves
with the monodromy group isomorphic to the Klein four-group. There are two cases, isotropic and
non-isotropic depending on the values of the Weil pairing restricted to the group defining the covering.
We recall from our previous work [4] the results concerning the non-isotropic case and fully describe the
isotropic case. We show that the necessary information to construct the Klein coverings is encoded in
the 6 points on P1 defining the genus 2 curve. The main result of the paper is the fact that, in both cases
the Prym map associated to these coverings is injective. Additionally, we provide a concrete description
of the closure of the image of the Prym map inside the corresponding moduli space of polarised abelian
varieties.

1. Introduction
Classically, Prym varieties are principally polarized abelian varieties arising from eÌtale double coverings
f : C 0 â†’ C between smooth curves. The Prym variety of such a covering is the complementary abelian
subvariety to the image of f âˆ— (JC) inside the Jacobian JC 0 . By extending this to the moduli space of
coverings one can define the Prym map to the moduli space Ag of principally polarised abelian varieties
(ppav). It is known that the Prym map is dominant for g â‰¤ 5 ([21]), so the general ppav of dimension
â‰¤ 5 is a Prym variety. Mumford revived the theory of Prym varieties by investigating in [16] the relations
between the Jacobian of C 0 and the Prym variety P (C 0 /C), which are known as the Schottky-Jung
relations. For g â‰¥ 7 the Prym map is generically injective but never injective ([5], [6], [8], [20]). The
study of the the Prym map for eÌtale double coverings of genus 6 curves led to the result that A5 is
unirational ([19]). These results inspired the research of Pryms for other coverings, which are no longer
principally polarised. The Torelli problem in the case of Pryms of ramified double coverings has been
fully solved in a series of recent papers [17, 14, 15] and the case of eÌtale cyclic coverings has been covered
in [12].
The main aim of this paper is to go a step further and study the most basic non-cyclic coverings,
namely eÌtale 4 : 1 coverings of genus 2 curves with monodromy group isomorphic to the Klein four-group.
These are examples of bidouble coverings but in the case curves we call them Klein coverings. Since the
group is non-cyclic, it turns out that the space of such coverings have two disjoint components depending
on the values of the Weil pairing restricted to the group defining the covering, called isotropic and nonisotropic. The non-isotropic case has appeared naturally in the investigation of smooth hyperelliptic
curves that can be embedded into abelian surfaces ([4]). One of the results of [4] is that such curves are
non-isotropic Klein coverings of genus 2 curves. In this paper we show that the corresponding Prym map
for non-isotropic Klein coverings over genus 2 curves is injective (see Theorem 3.8).
The isotropic case is quite different from the non-isotropic case since the covering is not longer hyperelliptic. The good side of the story is that we get more non-trivial quotient curves and in particular we
have been able to show that the Prym variety of the covering is the image of the Jacobian of one of them.
This allows us to show that in this case, the Prym map is also injective (see Theorem 4.11).
Putting the two theorems together we get the main result of the paper as follows.
