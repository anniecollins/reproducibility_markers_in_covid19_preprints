Abstract

16

In recent years, Zika spread through the Americas. This virus has been linked to

17

Guillain-BarrÃ© syndrome, which can lead to paralysis, and microcephaly, a severe birth

18

defect. Zika is primarily transmitted by Aedes (Ae.) aegypti, a mosquito whose geographic

19

range has expanded and is anticipated to continue shifting as the climate changes.

20

We used statistical models to predict regional suitability for autochthonous Zika

21

transmission using climatic variables. By suitability for Zika, we mean the potential for an

22

outbreak to occur based on the climateâ€™s habitability for Ae. aegypti. We trained zero1

24

25

194

195

196

197

199

200

201

616

In summary, the two top performing models perform well in terms of having

617

acceptable model coefficients, predicting at least one Zika case for all regions that

619

aegypti. Both models were able to predict suitability for Ae. aegypti and in turn Zika

620

outbreaks for the chosen subset of regions.

621

First, we compared the historical averages of the climatic variables used in the

622

model to the values of these variables from 2015-2017, when the Zika outbreak in the

623

Americas occurred. The mean deviations of the 2015-2017 observations from the

624

historical averages were relatively small (between 1 and 2 ÂºC) for the variables

625

winter.mean, summer.mean, year.mean, and year.diff. The average yearly mean

626

temperature was higher in the years 2015-2017 than it was in 1980-2005. For year.min,

627

the mean deviation from the historical average almost 3 ÂºC, and for year.max, the mean

628

deviation was just over 3 ÂºC. On average, year.min and year.mean were higher and

629

year.diff was lower in the years 2015-2017 than in the years 1980-2005, all changes

630

making regions more conducive to Zika outbreaks. None of the selected models used the

631

variable year.max, so the deviation in this variable is not concerning. For year.prec, the

632

mean deviation was large: almost 1000mm. Most of the places that reported Zika were

633

much drier between 2015 and 2017 than they were between 1980 and 2005, which is

634

unsurprising given the drought reported in Brazil the year than Zika broke out. Since

635

year.prec was not used in any of the selected models, the deviation between the climatic

636

average yearly precipitation and the yearly precipitation in 2015-2017 should not impact

637

the performance of the models.

638

Generally, the ZINB models outperformed the ZIP models. We speculate that the

639

additional variable to deal with over dispersion in the ZINB models contributed to the

640

performance differences. Concerning the performances of the top two models with Zika-

Future Considerations

672

Moving forward, these regions especially should prepare by educating citizens

673

about ways to prevent Zika infections, such as wearing long sleeves, using screens on

674

doors and windows, and using an EPA-registered insect repellant [63]. Economically

675

disadvantaged areas with poor access to water from pipes should work on more secure

676

water storage methods to reduce the creation of Ae. aegypti breeding grounds in water

677

containers [64]. Research institutions in these places should record more sightings of Ae.

678

aegypti and Ae. albopictus and look more into the potential for Ae. albopictus to become

679

as much or more of an issue than Ae. aegypti in spreading Zika. For example, if the

680

increasing populations of Ae. albopictus mosquitoes that have been driving down Ae.

681

aegypti populations in the US develop as strong a preference for human blood as Ae.

682

aegypti or start to bite primarily inside houses as well, they could become the primary

683

vector for Zika. As these mosquitoes already can survive and reproduce in cooler

684

climates, this could potentially cause more of an issue for Northern regions. In addition,

685

since less is known about the impacts of temperature on Ae. albopictusâ€™s feeding and

686

flying habits, more research should be conducted in that area as well.

687

The BCCCSM1.1 RCP85 climate projections are based on our current trajectory,

688

the highest emissions scenario, which results from not significantly reducing GHGs.

689

These projections suggest that many regions may become more hospitable for Ae.

690

aegypti, and many regions that have not seen autochthonous Zika cases before may see

691

them in the future. If actions are taken to reduce GHGs, less severe changes may occur

692

than those projected under this scenario, possibly reducing the risk for Zika to spread to

693

new regions.

694

In future research, we could use the weather data from the years that the Zika

695

outbreak in the Americas occurred to build the models. Although this data is not available

696

from stations in some of the selected cities in regions that observed Zika (Quito, Ecuador;

697

Georgetown, Guyana; Cayenne, French Guiana; and Port De Paix, Haiti), we could look

698

for the necessary data from other stations in those countries, or we could use historical

699

averages for those regions only. Also, we could increase the temporal resolution of these

700

modelsâ€™ predictions. Given that monthly or seasonal Zika data is available for enough

701

regions, we might accomplish this by predicting the number of Zika cases per season

702

between the beginning of 2015 and the end of 2017 using the climate data of each season

703

and the season before it [14]. We would also look for socioeconomic data for each region,

704

since socioeconomic factors can contribute to the creation of breeding grounds through

705

water storage methods [64]. We could also see how Ae. aegypti density and population

706

density perform as regressors, as they were useful variables in the research of Lo and

707

Park [36]. As a different method of projecting suitability for Zika outbreaks, we could model

708

incidence rate or percentage of population infected, like Huber, Caldwell, and Mordecai

709

had done, rather than modeling cases and including population as one of the regressors
