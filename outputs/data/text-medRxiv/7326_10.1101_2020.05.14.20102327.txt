1. Introduction
Recent studies indicates that aerosol transmission of the severe acute respiratory

42

syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2) is plausible since the virus can remain viable and

43

infectious in aerosol form for hours (1,2). Wearing personal protective equipment (e.g. N95

44

respirator) could be an effective strategy before the effective vaccines or antiviral drugs. The

45

N95 grade respirator should have a minimum filtration efficiency of 95% for ‚â•300 nm

46

(aerodynamic diameter) sodium chloride (NaCl) aerosols. Due to the COVID-19 pandemic

47

and shortage of mask supplies (3), some studies analyzed the reuse of N95/KN95 respirators

48

after sterilization by measuring the filtration efficiency using NaCl test aerosol (4,5).

49

However, in the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) National Institute of

50

Occupational Safety and Health (NIOSH) procedure No. TEB-APR-STP-0059, the count

51

median diameter (CMD) of the NaCl test aerosol is 75 nm ¬± 20 nm with the geometric

52

standard deviation (GSD) ‚â§1.86, which might be too small compared to the particles

53

containing infectious agents.

54

The peak concentration of the SARS-CoV-2 in hospitals was found in 250 nm ‚Äì 500

55

nm (aerodynamic diameter) particles (6), although the actual SARS-CoV-2 size ranges only

56

from 60 nm ‚Äì 150 nm (7,8,9). Since the most penetrating particle size (MPPS) for typical

57

N95 respirators is 30 nm ‚Äì 100 nm (10), the CDC NIOSH recommends using the NaCl test

58

aerosol with a CMD of 75 nm ¬± 20 nm and GSD of ‚â§1.86. However, using this size NaCl

59

test aerosol means that around 89.9% particles are ‚â§250 nm (aerodynamic diameter), which is

60

not in the particle size range of containing the peak concentration of the SARS-CoV-2 in

61

hospitals. The light-scattering instruments (e.g., aerosol photometer), which are commonly

64

65

66

67

68

The goal of this demonstration study was to compare the size specific filtration

69

efficiency to the overall filtration efficiency through testing an untreated (new) KN95, and

70

treated (multiple sterilization cycles) KN95/N95 respirators.

2.1 Experimental Setup

74

We tested KN95 respirators from over 20 various manufacturers, and selected two of

75

them (one qualified and one unqualified) for analysis in this study. We analyzed the reasons

76

why the unqualified KN95 respirator (Nine Particles Medical Equipment Co, Ltd, Yongzhou,

77

Hunan, China) still passed the test using the NIOSH recommended NaCl test aerosol. We

78

also compared the effects of two- and three-cycle sterilization using vaporous plasma

79

hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) and ultraviolet (UV) germicidal irradiation on the filtration

80

efficiencies by size of KN95 (qualified one from Civilian Antivirus, Qingdao, Shandong,

81

82

was illustrated in previous study (12). A NaCl aerosol was generated using a Collision

4. Discussion

156

This study proposed that the current NaCl test aerosol size might need to be increased

157

for testing the new KN95 respirator, and the reuse of N95 level respirators after sterilizations

158

due to their much larger MPPS compared to traditional N95 respirators‚Äô. In addition to

159

considering the overall filtration efficiency, the filtration efficiencies for particle sizes similar

160

to infectious particles should be considered. This study also found that the two different

161

multi-cycle sterilization processes have unique effects on the filtration efficiencies by aerosol

162

size of different respirators. Multi-cycle sterilization with UV appears to have fewer negative

163

effects than H2O2. Limitations include the small variety of respirator manufacturers and the

164

limited numbers of samples (n=5) for each respirator and only two sterilization techniques

165

evaluated. In addition, this study only evaluated the filtration efficiency after three

166

sterilization cycles as this corresponds with guidance from the American College of Surgeons

167

for H2O2 sterilization. The filter material might degrade further with more cycles, which
